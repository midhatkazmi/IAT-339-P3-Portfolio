Duolingo App Feature

  • Course: Interface Design
  • When: February 2018
  • Group: Sabrina Lee
  • Role: Research / wireframing
  • Tools: Adobe Illustrator / XD

Background

In this course, the project was to choose an app to research user problems and come up with a solution feature. This spanned over three weeks, the first consisting of user research, proposing three features in the second week, and choosing and refining one of the previous three features in the final week. Our group chose the Duolingo app and came up with the personal word bank feature.

A primary pain point that caused frustration for our persona (created from user research) was that words they were having difficulty with came up infrequently, making them unable to recall the word on a regular basis that would allow them to memorize it.

User Flows

Challenges

The main challenge of this project was creating the persona that resulted from our user research. We observed trends in users who were similar and struck a balance between keeping and discarding certain information. The result was a specific persona that the solution feature was based off of and applicable to.

Another challenge was consolidating our feature into one distinct feature. This was done by observing the primary function of the feature (to access personally chosen words) and adding controls that would be necessary to use this function.
